# Design Context

## Users

Late-night focusers, light sleepers, and people who open a tab just to drown out the world. They reach for this in low-light environments — headphones at midnight, a quiet office at 6 a.m., a hotel room they can't stand the silence in. The interface is consumed in glances, not studied.

## Brand Personality

**Three words: warm · mechanical · opinionated.**

The product is an object, not an app. It feels like a single piece of stereo equipment a careful person left on a shelf — pre-amber lamp glow, panel labels, a tactile play button you press with your thumb. Confident, never sterile, never apologetic. Reads more "1976 hi-fi catalog" than "2025 wellness startup."

## Aesthetic Direction

**Cassette / analog warmth.** Deep coffee-roast background with a paper-grain texture, warm lamp-amber accent, monospaced display caps, segmented VU-style level indicator. The visual surface is a single floating "deck" card. No skeuomorphic noise (no fake screws, no shiny gradients) but enough hi-fi vocabulary — knurled edges, tick marks, segmented meters — that the product reads as a *thing*, not a *screen*.

**Anti-references:**
- Generic wellness apps (cream + serif + terracotta + breathing circle)
- "Tech-y" black + neon
- iOS frosted glass / glassmorphism
- Heavy skeuomorphism (no fake wood grain, no faux brass screws)

**Theme: dark.** Late-night context, low-light viewing, headphone listening. Light theme would betray the mood.

## Design Principles

1. **The deck is one object.** Everything sits inside a single elevated panel with consistent material. No floating chips, no orphaned controls.
2. **Light is the language of state.** Playing = amber lamp on. Paused = lamp resting. State changes through illumination, not iconography.
3. **Mono caps are for the machine, proportional is for the human.** Labels, numbers, indicators in monospace. Any prose (none currently) in proportional.
4. **One signature moment.** Bloom-on-press / ember-on-release. Not scattered micro-animations.
5. **Tactile via type and edges, not via gloss.** Knurled bevels and tick marks earn their place; drop-shadows-with-glow do not.
